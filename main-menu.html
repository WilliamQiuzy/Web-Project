Traditional Cantonese Food

Guangdong is a coastal city in southern China, geographically close to Hong Kong. Cantonese food tastes relatively light. Cantonese people pay great attention to the freshness of ingredients, so most of the cooking methods in Guangdong are steaming or boiling.

Traditional Cantonese Architecture

China is most famous for its architecture. Chinese architecture is distinguished mainly by geographical location: south and north. Because it's rainy in the south and dry in the north. This page mainly displays Lingnan architectural style buildings and arcades with Guangdong characteristics.















TraditionalCantonese food:

  • Siu Mai

    Shaomai is a traditional Cantonese breakfast. It is a snack steamed with hot noodles (boiled water and semi-cooked dough) filled with pork, shrimp, crab roe and crab roe.

  • Claypot rice

    Traditional staple food in Guangdong. Cook the pork, eggs, vegetables and rice in a clay pot.

  • Roast chicken

    The chicken, which has been marinated for three hours in advance, is roasted in an airtight oven (earth kiln) until the skin is crisp.

  • Milk custard

    Traditional desserts of Guangdong. The finished milk is made in two layers, a thin milkshake on top and a custard with a pudding-like texture below.

  • Shrimp dumpling

    A traditional snack of Hong Kong and Guangdong. Minced shrimp or whole shrimp are wrapped in a very thin wrapper and then steamed.

  • Barbecued pork

    Roast the marinated pork. It's similar to American barbecue, but because of the different sauce, the flavor of barbecued pork is more salty and sweet.

  • Roast duck

    It's similar to roasting chicken, but to make the skin crispy and remove excess subcutaneous fat the chef drips hot oil over the duck gradually once it's cooked.

  • Steamed vermicelli roll

    A traditional breakfast for Cantonese is made of rice wrapped in a variety of meats and vegetables. The dough becomes wrinkled as it is folded multiple times.
